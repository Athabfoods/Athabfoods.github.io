Plant health:

There is much to learn in order to grow a bountiful garden easily and with no headaches.

Doing it extraordinary requires dedication to observe closely your plants and the life in your garden. To also take measurements periodically and then to slowly adjust over some years to come.

To do that one should understand most of the following topics:

  • Carbon Cycle

  • Composting

  • Ph

  • Watering with dissolved oxygen

  • Soil

  • Seed germination

  • Plant growth condition requirements like soil type, how much to water, fertilizer suplements, amount of sunlight, suseptability to rot, does it attract pests or benificals, how to mitigate pest attractors, etc. are all discoverable on line with a quick search. Make sure to have plant species and your question, find a reliable source and go with it. I like to use the American Government, something called local "state extension office. Or any other one. Here in Alberta I'd use the North Dakota extension office.

  • Pests

  • Friendly insect life in the garden

  • Air, Temperature and Humidity controling

  • Amending soils, top dressing beds, fertilizers, composting, etc.

  • Sunlight and lighting in general (if growing inside)

  • Keeping Roots Healthy

  • Roots Need Oxygen Too
  • Nitrogen Cycle

  • Nitrogen Fixing

  • Cation Exchange

  • Monitoring Runoff, and other testing

  • Plant Health

  • Microscope use for monitoring and recording the progression of soil life, seeing what stage of composting your heap is at, and whether or not there is bad or good microbes being produced

  • Collecting earth worm castings from a worm bin

  • HVAC (Ventilation)

  • Pruning

  • And more

Something I have come to appreciate about plants is that the healthier they are the less you have to worry about:

  • pests,
  • growth issues,
  • fertilizers,
  • pH-ing,

  • watering too much or too little .

Soil health and plant production:

Where does plant health come from?

Some say it comes from fertilizer while others say the soil .

In a way, both are correct however, fertilizer is simply nutrients extracted from something else and nutrients can be extracted from soil by microorganisms like nemotodes, protozoa, and the likes (This is very imprtant information for understanding soil Microbes and Nutrient Recycling, from Ohio State University).

This type of soil has many names; Living Soil, Veganic Soil, Supersoil , Mycorrhizal Fungi (IMPORTANT) and Soil, Substrate, Amended Soil , among some names.

In a way the soil is fertilizer because the plants health comes from the fact that the soil is digested by microbe life and the dirt it self is the food for that life.

Intrestingly enough, most soils that are producing poorly are not in need of amending but instead just in need of having microbe life revitalized.

Why does soil become life less over time? Monoculture (IMPORTANT).
