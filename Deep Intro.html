We go to the store many more times for food then we do for furnature, clothes, dishes, vehicles, insurace, school stuff, etc. Yes some of those things cost more, thats not the point.

What I am saying is that the largest volume of products that gets moved around globally IS food.

Meaning that food has the most land to grow on ONLY in places that get cold enough every year to hault major plant production.

Of the land that does grow food year round. Places like South America, South Africa, and South China (our bigger global producers) are either crowded with people, dense jungle (which should be protected), too mountainous, not very wide East to West, or usually some combo.

Winter and food growth:

Food can not grow when the ground is frozen, that is a fact. This is probably the biggest contributor to food costs globally, hands down.

Greenhouse growing is even more expensive the further north you go. This is due to cold winters combined with heating costs, the longer and more bitter the winter, ie: the further north you go, the more hours of night you see which meams you will have to spend more to heat a greenhouse.

This makes producing food year round in a greenhouse unfeasible and thus a southern thing.

Or a very expensive thing if choosing to do so and that is because of greenhouse design NOT location.

Greenhouses are designed to grow as many square feet of food as posible under one glass roof so their design is often a huge rectangular building.

This is great for summer sollar gain and equally as awesome at losing heat at night and in the winter. Not so good at keeping the food costs down.

Since the costs of energy keep on the rise, we have a recipe for food poverty that keeps growing.
