How do we know that we can grow Citrus and other warm climate foods here north of Edmonton in Alberta Canada?

Check this guy out near olds Alberta.

Make sure to see just how warm his greenhouse is shortly after Christmas, in Alberta 2021. This was a very very cold month (and winter) up here. -30 average for most of both December and January.

He manages close to 25C. This is from the Suns energy alone and using a greenhouse cover at night to keep the heat in.

This is without any sort of special attention to detail like burying the greenhouse into the ground or having the summer heat stored for cold winter usage.

Which is next...

Nebraska retire grows Oranges in the snow and here.

This shows how a simple thermal collection system can stabalize temperatures year round.

This is for less then a dollar a day in power. That is all the energy input he uses. He says 50 cents a day which equals $182.50 per year to heat and cool the entire greenhouse! IMPRESSIVE!

And look at the plants he can grow because of it... citrus too...

But this greenhouse is still above ground, and his heat is gathered from out in the yard which is outside the greenhouse footprint meaning the winters impact the long term residual heat that can be collected.

In my design we are storing our heat under the greenhouse floor so that over the years the temerature starts to rise up through the dirt and so that it is contained within the foundation walls.
