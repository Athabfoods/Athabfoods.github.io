Until then we use sugar (de-sulfurated molasses) water with a humic acid blend and/or more inoculant / brew once a month.

Plants and Oxygen:

Did you know that plants require Oxygen also?

They do. Not much, but they do.

In nature, it usually happens when rain falls. As raindrops fall they become concentrated with O2, when a drop hits the ground it takes that trapped oxygen into the ground with it. This oxygenates the roots which helps the roos do their routine better as well asding to the other positive effects.

Why? Oxygen promotes the roots to help plants process starches and sugars more efficiently. We all know how esential sugars are to photosynthesis. Well photosynthesis has another waste besides Oxygen, it's starch and thus very importat to process well.

One single plant breath:

A plant sort of breathes once per day.

During the day oxygen is exchanged out the leaves while absorbing carbon dioxide.

Roots need Oxygen too:

This happens while the roots take in Oxygen and expel CO2.

During the night most plants stop its oxygen production while the roots turn day time produced starches back into sugars and other things the plant needs.

Lightnings effects on rain:

Lightning atomizes the air with every strike. There are studies that suggest this helps sterilize the water by atomizing the atmosphere around rain as it falls.

The free part:

The actual growing of a plant is done by the soil, the water and the sunlight. We actually do nothing there. What we have to do is plant the seeds or seedlings, weed throughout the year and then harvest. The rest, besides watering, should be free.

Reducing other costs:

Weeding time:

Time weeding is mitogated by top dressing the base of plants or using filter cloth weed stop. You can also use cardboard to stop weeds. Cardboard eventually becomes part of the soil feeding it slightly.

For plants just cut holes in the above weed stop material that are slightly bigger than the trunk or stem will be This reduces the area needing to be weeded significantly, saving many hours of time.

Another way to slow weeds is with wood chips, shredded bark, grass clippings, or raked up yard leaves.

Seeding time:

You can use an auto seeder or hand held seeder to save time.

Harvesting time:

Microgreens harvester. Different hand held tools. Automated harvesting drones.

Watering time:

Automated watering with in ground sensors. Run aquarium bubbler in water at all times. Store water in opaque tanks that are in direct sun light for UV steralization.

Watering costs:

Collect rain water and snow runoff from buildings. Dehumidify water alongside. Water wells. Land run off diversion and collection then use rampumps to pump water for free. Or solar power and batteries to pump as back up.

Energy costs:

Produce electricity on site and store it. See energy section for more information on this.

Artificial lighting:

There is one form of efficent HID lighting, caled plasma lights. These beauties are super efficient. They do not have to be close to a plant to be ablke to cast enough light to the branches. They can hang on the roof and provide light through the entire depth to the ground with no need for multiple lights side by side.

Over watering:

Some plants can not tollerate being wet for extended periods. These plants get root rot easily and must be prevented at all costs.

Be sure to check your plant variety online or at an extention office website to see if your variety can handle base dressings.

*DON'T let any top dressing touch stems of plants or tree trunks. Always keep back 3+ inches and only an inch or two thick.

Compost tea:

Compost tea is a brew made from a handful or two of good compost, placing that in a large barrel with water, some form of food for the microbes and letting that brew in the sun for a week or two. The result is a root innoculent that seeds soil life while vastly improving plant growth for a period of time afterwards.

Compost tea can be made cheeply, it costs significantly less than fertilizers and is sprayed just as easily using similar equipment.
