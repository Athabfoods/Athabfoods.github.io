Plants and soil science. What is it?:

  • Mirobes. Some people call them 'myco's' or 'mycohyzzyl' they are a type of fungi (mushroom).

  • How to source these microbes? Compost tea, humus, compost, store bought inocculant cocktail, micro brew, etc. are all excellent sources of soil life. When we put these things in our soil we say we are 'inoculatibg it'.

  • Microbes need to eat something, so at first we need to give them food until they get going. For that we use sugar (de-sulfurated molasses) in water and humic acid blend and/or more inoculant / brew once a month.

  • As long as the root zone and the soil immediately around the plant are not disturbed the microbes will take hold and stay for significantly longer. Rototilling breaks the networks apart making them have to start all over again.
  • Plants also need good water.

  • Artificial lights work but are really expensive if paying for the equipment and/or the power bill.

  • A person should also monitor the propogation of microbes throughout the soil with a microscope. see microscope section. Coming soon.

Plants and Oxygen:

Did you know that plants require Oxygen?

They do. Not much, but they do.

In nature, it usually happens when rain falls. As raindrops fall they become concentrated with O2 (oxygenated). When a drop hits the ground it takes that trapped oxygen into the ground with it. This oxygenates the roots which helps the roots do their routine more efficiently. This adds other positive effects to the plant as wel.

Why? Oxygen promotes the roots to help a plant process starches and sugars more efficiently. We all know how essential sugars are to photosynthesis. Photosynthesis has another waste product besides Oxygen, it's starch. Starch is mostly processed at night making this important because plants slow down at night but have a whole days worth of starch to process for the next day.

One single plant breath:

A plant breathes once per day.

During the day, oxygen is exchanged out the leaves while absorbing carbon dioxide. Starting the breath cycle (inhalation). At night the roots take in Oxygen and expel CO2. Completing the full breath by exhalation.

Lightning has effects on the rain:

Lightning atomizes the air with every strike.

There are studies that suggest this helps sterilize water by atomizing the atmosphere around the rain as it falls.

That about completes what we can do to promote better growth through the root zone. There are other things we can do but their cost is not worth doing them.

Reducing costs by catagory:

Weeding:

Time weeding is mitogated by top dressing the base of plants or using filter cloth or weed stop. You can also use cardboard to stop weeds. Cardboard eventually becomes part of the soil feeding it slightly.

For the plants simply cut holes in the above weed stop materials that are slightly bigger than the trunk or stem will be. This reduces the area needing to be weeded significantly, saving many hours of time in a year.

Another way to slow weeds down is with wood chips, shredded bark, grass clippings, or raked up yard leaves.

Seeding:

You can use an auto seeder or a hand held seeder to save time.

Harvesting:

Microgreens hand held harvester. Different hand held tools. Automated harvesting. Drones.

Watering:

Automated watering with in ground sensors and condition monotoring systems. Run an aquarium bubbler in water at all times for O2. Store water in opaque tanks that are in direct sun light for UV steralization.

Water:

Collect rain water and snow runoff from buildings. Dehumidify water. Water wells. Land run off diversion and collection then use rampumps to pump water for free. Solar power and batteries pump backups.

Energy:

Produce electricity on site and store it. See energy section for more information on this (under construction).

Artificial lighting for season extension:

There is one type of HID lighting, called plasma lights. These beauties are super efficient. They do not have to be close to a plant like other HIDs. They cast enough light through the branches. They can hang on the roof and provide lumens through the entire depth of the branches to the ground with no need for multiple lights side by side saving immensely on power bills. Use energy generation and storage to help with the costs.

Root rot:

Some plants can not tollerate being wet for extended periods. This is how plants get root rot easily and must be prevented at all costs. Thankfully living soil is amazing at preventing this except in extreme situations.

Be sure to check your plant varieties online or on an extention office website to see if your variety has any thing you should know before you plant them.

Top dressing:

Top dressing helps retain moisture longer, keeps weeds down, prevents bugs, helps fertilize, protects against the hot sun drying the base of your plant out. But top dressing is sketchy at times. Check online to see if you should or should not top dress your roots.

*If you do, DON'T let any top dressing touch the stems of plants or tree trunks. Always keep back 3+ inches and only apply an inch or two thick.

Compost tea:

Compost tea is a brew made from a handful or two of good compost, placing that in a large barrel with water, some form of food for the microbes and letting that brew in the sun for a week or two. The result is a root innoculent that seeds soil life while vastly improving plant growth for a period of time afterwards.

Compost tea can be made cheeply, it costs significantly less than fertilizers and is sprayed just as easily using similar equipment.
