  • labour at warehouses outside of local region and at shipyards (x2)

  • all that cold storage costs are extra outside local area

  • the fuel to drive all forklifts, trucks and ships as well as the resources associated to build all of it

For most greenhouses add the following costs and remove the above costs:

  • power
  • gas (or electric heat)
  • yearly shutdown and startup = many unproductive months
  • daytime cooling
  • water
  • fertilizer or renewing soil
  • pesticides when needed are usually needed for the whole place = $$$
  • others

Fortunately almost all of these costs can be eliminated with a Strategic Greenhouse Systems design.

Worldwide motivating factors:

  • Floods do not affect a greenhouse like they do open field crops.


  • Children and people are starving. To help, food needs to be able to make it through extremes of weather and to grow for more months a year.


  • Insurance costs go up with catastrophic damage which further creates more costs for our food.


  • Food waste average in Canada is huge - over 60% farm to fork. Most of this waste, 90%, happens before the grocery store shelves - which means between farm and outside local is where most waste happens.

    In other words, long distance transportation is the leading cause of wasted food.

    If true then changing to regional production, or even in country production would reduce food waste by as much as 90%.


  • Don't forget the costs of energy keeps going up. We intend to mitigate that problem completely with on site energy production fueled by the wastes we create.


  • Temperature fluxuations and unpredictable weather are decimating food randomly. Some years are bad, others not so much. We need a way around this.


  • In holland they have huge areas that are greenhouses. Some areas it goes on for many square kilometers. So it not insane to think we can bring home close to year round growth and make a diffeence.

    The cost to maintain insurance and endure random crop losses is mitigated by indoor growth.


  • Plant health is better becasue of UV light. UV light is mostly blocked by typical greenhouse glazings.*** EFTE does not block it, ETFE is self cleaning, super light, and never fades. This is according to Japanese gardeners who have had ETFE on their greenhouses for over 30 + years now, with no fade or deterioration at all.


  • A man in Nebraska grows oranges year round for 50 cents a day and that is it. He keeps the ambient temperature well over 20C year round and does so by blowing air into tubes that go out into his yard and collect ground temp and bring it back in (on cold days). On hot days he collects the hot air and blows it through the same tubes which is now cooler and that cools the greenhouse while capturing that extra heat into the ground for later use.
