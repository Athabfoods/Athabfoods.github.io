Living soil:

Did I say living soil? I sure did. In fact the soil is teeming with life. Some say that soil is so diverse in microbes that for every tablespoon there is billions of individual living somethings.

And all those somethings must do something right?

... to be honest, were still working on it. We can say with certainty that having soil life can greatly impact plant life positively as much as not having soil life can be not good for plants.

How not good? Well that is complicated. You see I have been discovering that it is not entirely the soil life that impacts how, and what, plants contribute to their environment.

Again, with certainty I can say that no soil life means a plant is more or less defenseless. However, what I do not understand is why adding certain amendments do the things they do, both to the plant and to root zone.

We will go deeper into this later. For now just know that all evidence thus far suggests that we can prevent plant most issues such as pests, fungus and the likes, as well as root issues and we can do it easily with ***myco inoculations. You see, there are billions of little guys in there and hundreds, if not thousands of types too, each with a special thing they digest, communicate, or signal back to the plant.

Plants:

Yes! you read that right. There are communications happening from the plants through the roots to the microbes and back. The plants send ***exudates which can signal anything from "I am sick and need such and such", to "I am deficient in this nutrient", to "I have a pest infection send ______ to help me flush it out". Some plants even know when their own seed is present. When that happens they can kill off an area of their own roots in order to ***allow the seed best chance to germinate.

Plants ***have memory and can react (like ***venus fly trap) to events in their environment.

About microbes:

Microbes... as I said, these are complicated. We will go much deeper into this later.

Nonetheless, intresting things happen to plants with certain amendments. Some seem to make it so the roots can ***fix nitrogen better.

The concepts - breif overview of planned projects:

So. First project is a simple greenhouse atfirst look. But it is not simple at all.

It will capture all daytime heat and store it underground in the floor of the greenhouse for redistribution when it is winter and especially those nights.

This greenhouse will test soil science thoroughly north of Edmonton, not only for viability, but also for possibility of extreme north aplications.

Energy. To grid or not to grid:

We will have multiple sources of renewable energy production on site.
Thermo-photo voltaic cells need sun and the sun isn't always shining so we decided on a combo with mini wind as well as a potential to utilize hydrogen fuel cells.
With energy storage. Perhaps the power wall or redox-flow batteries. Or a combination of both. For those particularly long winter nights up here.
Energy has to be made on site to prevent impacting food costs any further.
Especially as energy always goes up, not down. If current long term trends hold true then it would not be good to neglect energy consumption mitigation.

Glazing and structure:

To benifit from natural ground warmth, the green house will be in the ground on the north side.

Because the glazing target is for EFTE this will replace what is typically lexan or glass which will be significantly less weight to hold up with the structure. This will benifit us nicely as we can now create verticall systems off of the roof supports along the column isles.
This will greatly increase our food output.

Since this is an in ground greenhouse, when going from south to north inside will be uphill in teirs. This is to allow sun penetration through the rows of trees to clear the leaves so as to create equal lighting throughout while maximizing food production space. Tomatoes in the ground and above rows upon rows of strawberries in auto vertical rotation planters.

To further create more layers of food production the rows of trees will all have rows of dwarf mangos and other dwarf varieties, as well as smaller plants to be plated in between trees for the rows running east west.

Water will be collected on the land and stored in outdoor dugouts on property. Rolling hills. Run off can easily be colllected in a water supply system, outlined later on.
