Before reading this page please make sure you checkout Why it will page which a has a good amount of the justification for this project.

Project vision:

To create a greenhouse system that allows for the growth of foods grown in warmer climates, foods typically grown tropically, subtropically, or temperate grown, and bring them into regions where these foods could never be grown. Like Edmonton, Alberta.

Project goals:

  1. -Use solar thermal energy storage to create a year round equal growing climate.

  2. -Use mothernature (see Nature magazine) as a teacher (David Suzuki- Nature of Things).

    Soil life matters, and so we try to replicate what she does in the wild into each and every garden pot we mix we make.


  3. -Utilize glazing products (glass) that promote more of the suns rays to penetrate.
    Being immensely lighter than glass and quite a bit lighter than various kinds of plastics (see ceres link), ethylene tetrafluoroethylene (ETFE) is by far one of the more superior choices for glazing a greenhouse.

    This is due to its properties as a greenhouse covering. It is an ultra light, self cleaning, able to be layered for insulating against the cold, as well as letting 99% of the suns ray spectrum in.

    Plants are used to being outside and grow the best outside because of the suns rays. Pests that are an issue in greenhouses are an issue partly because of the fact that other glazings can block UVA,
    UVB, and UV.
    Some combination of them or all of them are blocked by typical glazings.


  4. -We will utilize renewable energy and various energy storage methods.

    There is a whole section on this planned for down the road


  5. -Research and collect data on soil life, proliferation (see soil life), sustainability (what are humic and fulvic acids), and what all of that does to plant health exactly, as well as recording results and keeping microscope slides, microscope photos and videos and other measurements along the way.


  6. -We will provide this information for free on the internet. Hence the cheap HTML coded website for now. (links to leanr how to build your own site in HTML)

  7. All lessons learned will be shared.

  9. Alongside results will be photos, videos, excel charts and what ever else we feel important that will make it easy for others to succeed in a garden, a greenhouse, or to start their own agri-business.
