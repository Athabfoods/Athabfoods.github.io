Soil health and plant production:

Where does plant health come from?

Some say it comes from fertilizer while others say the soil .

In a way, both are correct however, fertilizer is simply nutrients extracted from something else and nutrients can be digested by microorganisms andhere (This is very imprtant information for understanding soil Microbes and Nutrient Recycling, from Ohio State University).

This type of soil has many names; Living Soil, Veganic Soil, Supersoil , Mycorrhizal Fungi (IMPORTANT) , Substrate, Amended Soil , among popular names.

In a way the soil is fertilizer because the plants health comes from the fact that the soil is digested by microbe life and the dirt it self is everything the plant needs it to be. It simply needs an intermediary (microlife in the soil) to get those things.

Intrestingly enough, most soils that are producing poorly are usually not in need of amending instead they are in need of microbe life revitalization.

Why does soil become life less over time? Monoculture (IMPORTANT).

How do we get life back into the soil?

It all starts with establishing sybiotic relationships between plants.

My experience has taught me that:

  1. Nutrients (fertilizers) are not quite equal to amendments in what they can accomplish. A few amendments costs less then fertilizing and way less then hydroponics set up and maintenance.

  2. Plants grow much more quickly once life is in the soil.

  3. They are now somehow immune to incredibly windy days.

  4. They no longer need supports for their heavy fruit like they used too (tomatoes, peppers, rhubarb, raspberries, strawberries, and apple trees to date).

  5. Fruit growth is maximized, something that makes harvest time a true joy to see.

  6. Veganic soil makes plants immune to frosts also. I have had hot pepper plants see a half dozen frosts with soil life and still have up standing leaves, with the actual peppers being firm and eddible still.

  7. Overwatering is not an issue either. We had a month where it rained over 600 mm, which is almost a years worth here and our plants still thrived during and after.

  8. Underwatering is something I know doesn't matter thanks to indoor growing. Fortunately, so far, I have not had to deal with outdoor droughts yet.

  9. I belive that this type of gardening has potential to solve real problems globally.

  10. I love veganic gardening.
